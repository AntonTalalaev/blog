Getting job in Google

Some useful tips for young specialists who eager to get a job in any leading IT company.

Posted by Anton Talalaev on November 29, 2020

Getting the job is always a very tricky thing to do, especially if you want to get a job at Google.

As for me, I would suggest to go through the shortlist above and do each step as best as you can.

So, this is my suggestions:

1) Do research about job requirements.
You will need to find open vacancies in Google company. You will need to check such aggregators as Indeed, network LinkedIn, and Google (or other aimed company) career page.
This work will give you knowledge about the desired stack of technologies, level of experience, professional certificates, and education.

2) Do your best to become the perfect candidate:
- learn technologies with which you are not familiar yet,
- try to get appropriate work experience,
- go through appropriate certifications.

3) Find Google employees on LinkedIn and research their accounts. You will need two things:
- to make your own account looks similar,
- to find certifications and courses that such professionals went through, and decide, do you need to do the same or not.

4) Based on made research, create a good resume and cover letter. Maybe you will need some help. There are lots of companies that provide such services.

5) In LinkedIn you will need to become a contactee of tech stack employees of Google, more - better. Just send requests.

6) After step 5, I suggest you try to get informational interviews, at least a few. You should think about questions a bit, google it.

7) You also can ask for help from your new contacts - to skim your resume, or even ask to send your resume to the recruiter team (a few times I was able to get such help, not in Google of course).

8) Search as much as you can about the interview process at Google. There are tons of data about it on the Web, also you can find a few books. And prepare hard.
