Heroes of Might and Magic 3

A few words about my favirite game.

Posted by Anton Talalaev on November 29, 2020

Necropolis is considered an imbalanced city and is often banned from tournaments. This stems from two whole ways of replenishing the army inaccessible to other castles:

- the resurrection of losing opponents in the form of skeletons after the battle (Necromancy Amplifier and special artifacts increases the number of raised creatures);
- the ability to turn creatures of any other faction into undead (Skeleton Transformer).

Also, there are two ways to resurrect units that have fallen in the battle in such a way that after the battle they remain in the army:

- casting Animate Dead spell will resurrect any unit of the dead army;
- Vampire Lords resurrect by damaging enemies.

Playing for Necropolis, I suggest you to aim to enhance the above advantages.

Some tips to consider

Hero tips

When choosing a hero, I advise you to choose Thant. This mage, with its enhanced Animate Dead, keeps troop losses in combat to a minimum. Thant casts Animate Dead with increased effect, based on his level compared to the level of the target unit.

Developing Thant as the main character you need to choose Earth Magic to increase Animate Dead spell effects. Deth army is very slow, to avoid such a disadvantage you need to take Logistics. Take Wisdom to cast high-level spells. Tactics is another good choice that will make your slow units (skeletons and zombies) more useful in the first minutes of each battle. In sum we have Necromancy (default), Mysticism (default), Earth Magic, Logistics, Wisdom. The other slots you can choose by yourself.

Skeleton Transformer tips

During the game, you need to have some heroes to collect free/chip units on the map. These units need to be transformed into sceletons using Skeleton Transformer. Also, think about skill Diplomacy for the second hero. This skill will allow you to get extra units that also can be transformed into skeletons. Another interesting tip is that if you will use any living dragon to transfer into a skeleton, you will get the Skeleton dragon.

The main army suggestions

For the main army I suggest:
Stage One: Skeletons, Power Liches, and Dread Knights;
Stage Two: Skeleton Warriors, Power Liches, Dread Knights and Vampire Lords;
Stage Three: Skeleton Warriors, Power Liches Dread Knights, Vampire Lords, and Ghost dragons.

Skeleton warriors 6/6/6/1-3/5/70

Based on the advantages of Necropolice, the numbers of this unit will be high. Numbers = strength. Skeleton warriors are raised after a battle like skeletons, but only if you don't have normal skeletons in the army and all slots are occupied.

Power Liches 13/10/40/11-15/7/600

Area attack is the main power of Power Liches.

Dread knights 18/18/120/15-30/9/1500

Good attack and defense stats. Can curse enemies. Chance to deathblows, which cause double damage. The strongest unit of Necropolice and one of the most powerful level 6 creatures in the game.

Vampire Lords 10/10/40/5-8/9/500

Enemies don't respond to attack. Use it wisely - attack strong enemies with Vampire Lords first. Vampire Lords resurrect by damaging enemies. You need to include Vampire Lords in your army only when you can afford a big number of them. 200 Vampire Lords is a very strong enemy.

Ghost dragons 19/17/200/25-50/14/3000 + 1m

Good speed. Lower morale of the enemy army. Can cast an aging attack that halves the enemy's health.

Necropolis is considered as one of the most powerful castles in Heroes of Might and Magic 3. It is also one of the most interesting castles to play. The advantages of Necropolis become stronger on bigger maps where you have time to generate an army of undead. Avoid using Necropolis on small maps (in short games). Be careful in the first stages of the game - in the first weeks Necropolis is quite weak - you can be easily defeated. Use Animate Dead wisely to limit unit losses, collect units, and you will win.
